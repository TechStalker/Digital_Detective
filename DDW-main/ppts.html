Intellectual property rights are a form of property that enable the owner to profit from their creation and prevent others from using or tampering with it without their consent.
What is IPR?
IPR (Intellectual Property Rights)
The International Declaration of Human Rights provides for the right to benefit from the protection of the moral and physical interests resulting from the right holder's work, whether it is a literal or artistic product. This is not a monopoly of the intellect, but rather the protection of the moral and physical interests resulting from the right holder's work.
Protecting IPR
Copyright is a form of IPR that protects works of human intellect, such as literary and artistic works, as well as technology-based works such as computer programs and electronic databases.
What is Copyright?
Pay attention to the symbols ™, ®, and ©



The TM symbol is used in conjunction with an unregistered mark to alert potential infringers that common law rights are asserted. It is often written in superscript and may not be protected by trademark rules. Even if a request to register the mark is turned down, the owner may still utilize the TM symbol.
(™)TradeMark
The ® symbol is used to signify the ownership of a trademark or service mark, allowing people to know who legally owns it. It can only be used once the mark has been registered .
(®) Registered TradeMark
© The owner of an intellectual property has the sole right, which is known as copyright. It guards against the work of the creator being copied or used without permission.
